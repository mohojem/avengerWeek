Hawkeye

Fun facts about Hawkeye

🏹 Hawkeye (Clint Barton)

Created by: Stan Lee and Don Heck

First Appearance: Tales of Suspense #57 (1964)

Strengths: Master archer, tactician, skilled in combat and acrobatics.

Weaknesses: Human limitations, poor impulse control in early comics.

Overlooked Fact: He was deaf in the comics for a long time—a fact reintroduced in the Disney+ series.

Inspiration: Robin Hood-style vigilante, originally mistaken for a villain.


He Was Deaf for a Huge Chunk of His History

In the comics, Clint lost most of his hearing early on (in Hawkeye Vol. 1 #4, 1983).

It happened when he used a sonic arrow to stop a villain—intentionally deafening himself.

He wore hearing aids for decades in the comics.

In later runs like Matt Fraction’s 2012 Hawkeye, this deafness was revisited and became central to his identity.

👉 Hawkeye is one of the few disabled heroes who doesn't have a “superpower workaround.


He Was a Villain First

He was manipulated by Black Widow (then a Russian spy) into fighting Iron Man.

After realizing his mistake, he switched sides and joined the Avengers.

📌 This puts Clint in the unique position of being an Avenger who chose redemption rather than being recruited or born heroic.
